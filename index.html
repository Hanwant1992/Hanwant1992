About Me

Welcome! I’m Dr. Hanwant Singh, a plant biologist with a deep interest in how plants survive and adapt to tough environmental conditions. My research focuses on plant stress physiology and biochemistry—studying how plants respond to challenges like heat, drought, and pollution. I use tools like chlorophyll fluorescence to study photosynthesis, the process plants use to turn sunlight into energy. This technique helps me understand how well plants are functioning, especially under stress. I also work in plant molecular biology, exploring the roles of specific genes that help plants tolerate stress. My goal is to use this knowledge to develop new, stress-tolerant plant varieties that can thrive in challenging environments. Through my work, I aim to contribute to sustainable agriculture and help address global food and climate challenges. For me, plants are not just subjects of study—they are powerful examples of patience, resilience, and quiet strength. I believe that by learning from them, we can find smarter, greener ways to live and grow.
